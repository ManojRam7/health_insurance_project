1. INTERVIEW SCRIPT – How to explain the Silver Members layer
────────────────────────────────────────────

“In this project, I built an enterprise-grade data pipeline for the insurance domain using a layered architecture: Raw → Bronze → Silver → (eventually Gold). One of the key entities is **Members**, which represents the insured individuals under each policy.”

Here’s how I explain the **Members Silver** layer to non-technical stakeholders:

🔹 Why do we even have a Silver Members layer? (Business reason)

The **Bronze Members** dataset is a direct landing of raw data coming from upstream (in this case, Kaggle + synthetic enrichment).

It often contains issues such as:

- Inconsistent or messy values for fields like Gender and Smoker  
- Implausible **Age** values  
- Out-of-range or suspicious **BMI** values  
- Free-text **Chronic_Disease** and **Employment_Status** with multiple spellings  
- Missing or invalid **Policy_ID** (foreign key)  
- Duplicated member records over time  

If we give this directly to reporting or data science teams, they will:

- Spend a lot of time cleaning data themselves, differently each time  
- Potentially use incorrect or inconsistent assumptions about what is valid  
- Risk producing KPIs or models that the business cannot fully trust  

The **Silver Members** layer solves this by being:

> “A cleaned, standardized, policy-linked view of every member, with data quality rules applied and features engineered for analytics.”

It is the **trusted source** for anything related to insured individuals.

🔹 What transformations did we perform? (Major + Minor)

Major Transformations  
(High-level logic applied in the Silver Members pipeline)

| Transformation                                  | Business Explanation                                                                                  |
|-------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1. Schema enforcement                           | Ensures every member record follows a standard structure — no missing or inconsistent core fields.    |
| 2. Primary/foreign key validation (Member_ID, Policy_ID) | Guarantees every member has a unique ID and is correctly attached to a valid policy.              |
| 3. Age plausibility checks                      | Prevents obviously incorrect ages (e.g. negative or > 110) from entering analytics.                  |
| 4. BMI plausibility checks                      | Flags extreme or impossible BMI values so clinical or risk analytics are not misled.                 |
| 5. Categorical normalisation (Gender, Smoker)   | Standardises messy values like `male/M/Male` into a consistent format (e.g. M/F, Y/N).               |
| 6. Deduplication using latest-record-wins       | Removes duplicated members while keeping the latest, most reliable record per Member_ID.             |
| 7. Policy foreign key integrity                 | Ensures members always point to an existing policy in Silver Policies; orphans are quarantined.      |

Minor but Enterprise-Critical Enhancements

In addition to cleaning, the Silver Members layer introduces **business-friendly fields and flags** (you can reference these or extend your code accordingly):

| Feature Added          | Why It Matters                                                                                 |
|------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Age_Band               | Groups Age into buckets (e.g. 18–25, 26–35, etc.) for easier segmentation and reporting.      |
| BMI_Category           | Converts BMI into categories (Underweight/Normal/Overweight/Obese) for risk and health views. |
| dq_age_valid           | Flags whether Age falls in a plausible range (e.g. 0–110).                                     |
| dq_bmi_valid           | Flags whether BMI falls into a medically plausible range (e.g. 10–60).                         |
| dq_fk_policy_valid     | Flags whether the member’s Policy_ID successfully matches a policy in Silver Policies.         |

These features make it effortless for analysts to filter to “only clean members” or segment members by risk, age, or region without reimplementing logic.

Other supporting logic

| Logic                           | Why It Exists                                                                                 |
|---------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Normalised Gender & Smoker      | Ensures consistent gender and smoking indicators across countries and source systems.         |
| Controlled Employment_Status    | Prevents proliferation of variants like `Self Employed / self-employed / Self-Employed`.     |
| Region cleaned and standardised | Simplifies geographic analysis and avoids “Region 01 vs 1 vs 001” ambiguity.                 |
| Quarantine layer                | Problematic members are preserved for audit, not silently dropped.                           |
| Metrics logging                 | Tracks row counts and distinct members for governance and pipeline observability.            |

🔹 How would I explain this to a business leader?

“In short, the Silver Members layer turns person-level data from ‘messy and unreliable’ into a **trusted, policy-linked, analytics-ready population**. It makes segmentation, risk analysis, and customer understanding accurate and repeatable, without every analyst reinventing data cleaning.”

🔹 What business problems does this solve?

- Prevents misleading insights from invalid Age/BMI values  
- Ensures every member is properly tied to a policy (no ghost records)  
- Reduces manual data cleaning effort in every project  
- Provides standardised definitions for Gender, Smoker, Region, etc.  
- Enables risk, health, and segmentation analyses on top of clean attributes  
- Gives clear visibility of data quality via DQ flags and quarantine

2. PRESENTATION SLIDE FORMAT (copy/paste into PowerPoint)
────────────────────────────────────────────

Below is a slide-by-slide structure ready for interview presentations.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Slide 1 — Project Overview

“Enterprise-Grade Data Pipeline: Bronze → Silver Members”

- Domain: Insurance Members (people covered under policies)  
- Feeds higher-level views: Claims, Policies, Risk, Health insights  
- Built using a Lakehouse-style architecture with Delta  

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Slide 2 — Why a Silver Members Layer?

Bronze Members = raw, unfiltered, unvalidated.

Typical problems:

- Missing or invalid Policy_ID
- Implausible Age or BMI values
- Messy Gender and Smoker values
- Inconsistent free-text Chronic_Disease and Employment_Status
- Potential duplicates for the same member

Silver Members:

> Provides a **clean, policy-linked, analytics-ready** view of all insured individuals.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Slide 3 — Silver Members Transformation Goals

- Standardise schema and data types  
- Enforce Member_ID and Policy_ID integrity  
- Check Age and BMI plausibility  
- Clean and normalise categorical fields (Gender, Smoker, Employment, Region)  
- Ensure each member is attached to a valid policy  
- Deduplicate records (one row per Member_ID)  
- Add business-friendly features and DQ flags  
- Persist metrics for monitoring row counts and quality  

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Slide 4 — Bronze → Silver: What Stays the Same

Core fields carried through (validated and type-enforced):

- Member_ID  
- Policy_ID  
- Age  
- Gender  
- BMI  
- Smoker  
- Chronic_Disease  
- Employment_Status  
- Region  

Message:

> “We keep the business meaning intact; we just clean and validate the data so it’s safe to use.”

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Slide 5 — New Fields / Enhancements in Silver

Examples of additional Silver-level features:

- **Age_Band**  
  - Age grouped into segments: e.g. 18–25, 26–35, 36–45, etc.  
  - Makes it easier to see age distribution in dashboards.

- **BMI_Category**  
  - Underweight / Normal / Overweight / Obese, derived from BMI.  
  - Supports risk and health-related analysis.

- **dq_age_valid**  
  - 1 if Age is within a plausible range (e.g. 0–110).  
  - 0 if clearly incorrect (negative, or unrealistically high).

- **dq_bmi_valid**  
  - 1 if BMI in plausible range (e.g. 10–60).  
  - Helps detect data entry or ingestion errors.

- **dq_fk_policy_valid**  
  - 1 if Policy_ID successfully matches a record in Silver Policies.  
  - 0 for orphaned members, which are quarantined or investigated.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Slide 6 — Data Quality & Governance

Checks performed include:

- Null or missing Member_ID / Policy_ID  
- Age outside plausible range  
- BMI outside plausible range  
- Gender/Smoker values not in controlled vocabulary  
- Members whose Policy_ID does not exist in Silver Policies  

Handling:

- Violating records are quarantined (written to a dedicated `_quarantine` area).  
- For critical rules, the pipeline can be configured to fail if violation percentage exceeds an agreed SLA.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Slide 7 — Deduplication Logic

- We ensure **one record per Member_ID** in Silver.  
- Rules can be “latest record wins” based on a timestamp or other ordering column.  
- Outcome: Clean population view without duplicate individuals.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Slide 8 — Categorical Normalisation

- Gender normalised to `M` / `F` where possible  
- Smoker normalised to `Y` / `N`  
- Employment_Status mapped to a controlled list (e.g. Employed, Self-Employed, Retired, Student)  
- Region cleaned into a consistent, joinable representation

Business impact:

- Ensures consistent segmentation and filtering across reports, teams, and models.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Slide 9 — Policy Foreign Key Integrity

- Silver Members is **joined against Silver Policies**  
- Members with invalid Policy_IDs are quarantined or flagged via `dq_fk_policy_valid`  
- Guarantees that member-level analysis is consistent with policy-level reporting.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Slide 10 — Metrics Logging

Automated metrics for observability:

- Total Silver members processed  
- Distinct Member_IDs  
- (Optionally) counts of quarantined members by reason  

These metrics are written to a central `_metrics` Delta table for monitoring.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Slide 11 — Business Value Summary

The Silver Members layer provides:

- ✔ A trustworthy, policy-linked member population  
- ✔ Clean Age, BMI, Gender, Smoker, and Region attributes  
- ✔ Ability to filter on quality (DQ flags)  
- ✔ Input for risk scoring, churn models, and health analytics  
- ✔ Strong governance (quarantine + metrics)  

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Slide 12 — Outcome

“The organisation now has a **clean, consistent, policy-aware Members dataset**. Business teams can use it confidently for segmentation, targeting, risk analysis, and member experience initiatives, without spending time re-cleaning data.”
