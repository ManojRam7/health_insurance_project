1. INTERVIEW SCRIPT — Claims Silver Layer Explanation
===============================================================

“In our Claims Silver layer, the objective was to transform messy,
semi-structured insurance claim records from their raw Kaggle sources 
into a clean, validated, analysis-ready dataset that upholds the same 
enterprise standards as a real Bupa, Aetna, AXA, or Aviva lakehouse environment.”

Just like the Policies and Members pipelines, Claims Silver enforces:

• Schema correctness  
• Primary key validation  
• Referential integrity checks  
• Date consistency  
• Financial sanity rules  
• Deduplication  
• Business-friendly derived columns  
• Data Quality flags  
• Quarantine-first handling of errors  
• Full traceability and auditability  

But Claims has some unique challenges:
the raw Kaggle datasets come from TWO different domains:

• U.S. Medicare Fraud dataset  
• Motor insurance cross-sell synthetic dataset  

Because these datasets were never meant to join perfectly,
we treat some relationships (like Member_Key) as *soft joins*
and others (Provider_ID) as *strong business keys*.

---------------------------------------------------------------
A. What issues did we see in Bronze Claims?
---------------------------------------------------------------

The raw Claims showed:

• 7% of claims missing Provider_ID  
• 100% mismatch between Member_Key and our synthetic Members dimension  
• Most Date_Reported and Date_Settled fields are NULL  
• Financial values are non-negative but unvalidated  
• No guarantee that Provider_ID referenced an actual provider  
• No business KPI fields such as settlement duration  
• No governance around invalid or suspicious claims  

Silver fixes or surfaces all of these issues.

---------------------------------------------------------------
B. Major Transformations in Silver Claims
---------------------------------------------------------------

Transformation                  | Business Explanation
------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------
Schema enforcement             | Ensures every claim follows a predictable structure.
Primary key validation         | Guarantees every claim is uniquely identifiable.
Provider FK integrity checks   | Ensures claims reference valid providers (7% fail → quarantined).
Member_Key soft validation     | Logged but not enforced because datasets come from different sources.
Date reversal validation        | Ensures Date_Settled never precedes Date_Reported.
Financial sanity verification  | Flags impossible payouts or claim amounts.
Deduplication                  | Removes repeated claim snapshots (latest preserved).
New KPI: Days_To_Settle        | Core claim performance metric for operations & governance.
DQ Flags                       | dq_money_valid, dq_date_valid give transparency on input quality.
Quarantine                     | All failures stored, none silently dropped.

---------------------------------------------------------------
C. Minor Enhancements (Enterprise-Critical)
---------------------------------------------------------------

Feature Added      | Why It Matters
------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------
Days_To_Settle     | Allows SLA monitoring, customer wait-time analysis.
dq_money_valid     | Flags corrupted payout/claim fields before they hit dashboards.
dq_date_valid      | Identifies suspicious lifecycle anomalies early.
Provider FK checks | Ensures claim-provider joins are safe in BI tools.

---------------------------------------------------------------
D. The Member_Key issue (Important interview topic)
---------------------------------------------------------------

Member_Key (from Medicare dataset) does NOT match Member_ID (from synthetic cross-sell dataset).  
This is *expected*.

So we treat Member_Key as:

• A source-system operational identifier  
• Not enforced as a foreign key  
• Not quarantined  
• Tracked for data lineage only  

This mirrors real enterprise integration, where legacy systems don’t join until a master-data layer is built.

---------------------------------------------------------------
E. Final Value Delivered to the Business
---------------------------------------------------------------

“We transformed raw, inconsistent claims into a governed, trusted dataset
that is safe for operational KPIs, financial monitoring, fraud analysis, 
and end-to-end customer experience metrics—even though the source data 
came from disconnected Kaggle systems.”
